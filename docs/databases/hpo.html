Human Phenotype Ontology — Standardized Phenotype Terminology

Home › Databases › HPO

Overview

The Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO) is a standardized, hierarchical vocabulary of human phenotypic abnormalities. It provides a comprehensive framework for describing clinical manifestations observed in diseases, linking phenotypes to diseases, genes, and variants. HPO enables systematic phenotype annotation and supports computational analysis of genetic diseases.

Structured Phenotype Vocabulary — HPO provides standardized terms for clinical observations, enabling precise phenotype annotation and computational phenotype-driven diagnosis and gene discovery.

Key Features

  • Standardized phenotype terms
  • Hierarchical organization
  • Disease associations
  • Gene-phenotype links
  • Multi-level hierarchy
  • Variant annotations

Use Cases

  • Describe clinical phenotypes
  • Find genes causing phenotypes
  • Diagnose genetic diseases
  • Prioritize candidate genes
  • Study disease similarity
  • Annotate genetic variants

Phenotype Search and Analysis

hpo_search

Search HPO terms and retrieve gene-disease-phenotype associations.

Parameters: term (string: phenotype term), gene (string, optional: gene symbol or Ensembl ID), disease (string, optional: disease name or OMIM ID)

Returns: HPO term ID, definition, synonyms, parent/child terms, associated genes, disease associations, frequency information

HPO Term Categories:

  • Phenotypic abnormality
  • Organ abnormality
  • Inheritance pattern
  • Clinical course
  • Mortality/Natality

Example Queries

Find phenotypes for a disease

"What are the clinical phenotypes associated with cystic fibrosis according to HPO?"

Tool: hpo_search with disease="cystic fibrosis"

Find genes causing a phenotype

"Which genes cause intellectual disability phenotypes in humans?"

Tool: hpo_search with term="Intellectual disability"

Link genes and phenotypes

"What phenotypes are associated with mutations in the BRCA1 gene?"

Tool: hpo_search with gene="BRCA1"

Study disease phenotype overlap

"Find common phenotypes between cancer and cardiovascular diseases in HPO."

Tool: hpo_search with multiple phenotype/disease queries

HPO Hierarchy & Organization

Hierarchical Structure

HPO is organized as a directed acyclic graph (DAG) where terms can have multiple parent and child terms. This allows flexible, multi-perspective categorization of phenotypes.

Main Phenotype Categories

  • Abnormality of blood: Anemia, leukopenia, platelet disorders
  • Abnormality of the nervous system: Seizures, developmental delay, neuropathy
  • Abnormality of the cardiovascular system: Cardiac defects, arrhythmias
  • Abnormality of the respiratory system: Cystic fibrosis, lung disease
  • Abnormality of metabolism: Metabolic disorders, lysosomal storage
  • Abnormality of the immune system: Immunodeficiency, autoimmunity
  • Skeletal abnormality: Bone dysplasias, limb malformations

Frequency Terms

HPO includes frequency modifiers indicating how common a phenotype is in a disease:

  • Very frequent: Present in > 80% of cases
  • Frequent: Present in 30-80% of cases
  • Occasionally: Present in 5-29% of cases
  • Rare: Present in < 5% of cases

Disease-Gene-Phenotype Network

Integrated Knowledge

HPO connects phenotypes to genetic diseases and disease-causing genes, enabling:

  • Phenotype-driven gene discovery
  • Clinical diagnostic support
  • Disease similarity assessment
  • Prioritization of candidate variants
  • Rare disease diagnosis

Data Sources

HPO integrates disease information from OMIM (Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man) and Orphanet, providing comprehensive coverage of Mendelian and rare genetic diseases.

Integration with Datasets MCP

HPO enhances clinical and genetic analysis:

  • With Ensembl: Annotate variants with disease phenotypes
  • With ClinVar: Link clinical assertions to phenotypic outcomes
  • With UniProt: Correlate protein function loss with phenotypic consequences
  • With PubMed: Find literature describing phenotypes and genetic associations
