Reactome

Curated Biological Pathway Database with Reaction-Centric Data Model

Home › Databases › Reactome

Overview

Reactome is a free, open-source curated database of biological pathways and reactions in humans, with support for model organisms through ortholog inference. It provides detailed mechanisms of human biological processes including signal transduction, gene expression, apoptosis, and metabolism. The reaction-centric data model captures molecular details and enables computational analysis.

Reaction-Level Curation — Reactome captures detailed reaction mechanisms with specific substrates, products, catalysts, and regulatory information, enabling mechanistic understanding of biological pathways.

Key Features

  • Curated reaction mechanisms
  • Pathway hierarchy and organization
  • Protein complex composition
  • Multi-organism support
  • Disease associations
  • Literature references

Use Cases

  • Understand biochemical pathways
  • Map genes to biological processes
  • Find disease-associated pathways
  • Study signal transduction
  • Analyze metabolic networks
  • Perform pathway enrichment

Pathway Query and Analysis

reactome_get_pathway

Retrieve pathway information, member reactions, and associated proteins.

Parameters: pathway_id (string: Reactome stable ID), species (string, optional: "Homo sapiens", "Mus musculus", etc.)

Returns: Pathway name, reactions, member proteins, complexes, regulation, disease associations, literature references

Supported Organisms:

  • Homo sapiens (Human)
  • Mus musculus (Mouse)
  • Rattus norvegicus (Rat)
  • Gallus gallus (Chicken)
  • Danio rerio (Zebrafish)
  • Caenorhabditis elegans (Worm)
  • Drosophila melanogaster (Fruit fly)

Pathway Organization & Structure

Hierarchical Organization

Pathways are organized hierarchically from broad categories (e.g., "Signal Transduction") to specific mechanisms. This allows both high-level overview and detailed mechanistic analysis.

Key Pathway Categories

  • Signal Transduction: Growth factors, G-protein signaling, protein kinases
  • Gene Expression: Transcription, translation, RNA processing
  • Metabolism: Carbohydrate, lipid, amino acid, and nucleotide metabolism
  • Immunity: Innate and adaptive immune responses
  • Apoptosis: Programmed cell death mechanisms
  • Cell Cycle: Checkpoint control and division regulation
  • Transport: Protein trafficking and cellular transport

Reaction Level Detail

Each reaction includes detailed information about inputs, outputs, catalysts, and regulatory factors, enabling computational modeling of pathway behavior and prediction of pathway consequences when proteins or genes are perturbed.

Integration with Datasets MCP

Reactome pathways integrate with other bioinformatics data:

  • With Ensembl: Map genomic variants to pathway genes and assess functional impact
  • With UniProt: Find protein interactions and modifications in pathway context
  • With ClinVar: Identify disease variants in pathway-critical proteins
  • With KEGG: Cross-validate pathway information and fill annotation gaps
