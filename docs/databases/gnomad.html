gnomAD

Population Allele Frequencies, Variant Quality, and Population Genetics

Home › Databases › gnomAD

Overview

gnomAD (Genome Aggregation Database) is a freely available database of variant frequencies from sequencing data of ~1 million unrelated individuals. It provides population allele frequencies, quality metrics, and constraint scores for all types of genetic variants, enabling better variant interpretation in clinical and research contexts.

Large-Scale Population Database — gnomAD combines exome and whole-genome sequencing data from diverse populations, providing unbiased frequency estimates for rare and common variants.

Key Features

  • Population allele frequencies
  • Exome and genome coverage maps
  • Variant quality metrics
  • Population stratification
  • Constraint and depletion scores
  • Coverage distribution

Use Cases

  • Filter common variants
  • Assess variant pathogenicity
  • Explore population differences
  • Identify population-specific variants
  • Evaluate constraint on genes
  • Check coverage in regions

Variant Frequency Lookup

gnomad_get_variant

Retrieve population allele frequencies for a genetic variant.

Parameters: variant (string: rsID, HGVS, or coordinates), population (string, optional: all, afr, ami, asj, eas, fin, nfe, sas, other)

Returns: Allele frequency, population breakdown, quality metrics, constraint scores, coverage

Population Codes:

  • afr — African
  • ami — Amish
  • asj — Ashkenazi Jewish
  • eas — East Asian
  • fin — Finnish
  • nfe — Non-Finnish European
  • sas — South Asian

Example Queries

Check variant frequency in populations

"What is the allele frequency of rs1234567 in gnomAD? Show population breakdown."

Tool: gnomad_get_variant with variant="rs1234567"

Filter common variants

"Is the variant chr1:1000000:A/G common in European populations in gnomAD?"

Tool: gnomad_get_variant with variant="chr1:1000000:A/G" and population="nfe"

Identify population-specific variants

"Show variants in the PCSK9 gene that are found only in African populations."

Tool: gnomad_get_variant with population="afr" filter

Assess variant rarity

"How rare is this variant (c.123A>G) in the general population according to gnomAD?"

Tool: gnomad_get_variant with variant="c.123A>G"

Understanding Allele Frequencies

Frequency Interpretation

  • AF > 5%: Common variant (usually benign)
  • 1% < AF ≤ 5%: Intermediate frequency
  • 0.1% < AF ≤ 1%: Rare variant
  • AF ≤ 0.1%: Very rare variant (likely pathogenic if damaging)
  • AF = 0: Absent in gnomAD (novel or ultra-rare)

Constraint Scores (pLI & Z-scores)

gnomAD computes constraint metrics reflecting how intolerant genes are to loss-of-function variants:

  • pLI ≥ 0.9: Highly constrained (intolerant of LoF mutations)
  • 0.1 < pLI < 0.9: Moderately constrained
  • pLI ≤ 0.1: Unconstrained (tolerant of LoF mutations)

Data Quality & Caveats

Coverage & Callable Bases

gnomAD provides per-base coverage information. Some regions may have low coverage, reducing sensitivity to rare variants. Always check coverage for regions of interest.

Population Representation

gnomAD includes diverse populations but may underrepresent some groups. Population-specific frequencies should be interpreted within context.

Filter Status

Variants are marked as "PASS" or filtered based on quality criteria. Always check filter status when analyzing data.

Integration with Datasets MCP

gnomAD integrates with clinical and population genetics workflows:

  • With ClinVar: Compare allele frequencies to clinical significance
  • With Ensembl: Filter variants by gnomAD frequency and consequences
  • With NCBI Datasets: Context genes with population variant data
  • With UniProt: Link frequent variants to protein regions
