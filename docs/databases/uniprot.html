UniProt

Protein Function, Sequences, Domains, Modifications, and Disease Annotations

Home › Databases › UniProt

Overview

UniProt is the universal protein knowledge base providing comprehensive, high-quality protein sequences, functions, and disease annotations for all known proteins across all organisms. It combines reviewed (Swiss-Prot) and unreviewed (TrEMBL) entries with powerful search and analysis tools.

Reviewed & Unreviewed Data — Swiss-Prot entries are manually curated and reviewed; TrEMBL entries are automatically annotated. Both are accessible through a unified search interface.

Key Features

  • Complete protein sequences
  • Functional annotations and GO terms
  • Domain and motif information
  • Post-translational modifications
  • Disease and variant associations
  • Protein interactions

Use Cases

  • Look up protein function and properties
  • Find protein domains and motifs
  • Identify post-translational modifications
  • Link variants to protein features
  • Discover disease-related proteins
  • Get subcellular localization

Protein Search & Annotation

uniprot_search

Search for proteins by name, gene symbol, or UniProt ID.

Parameters: query (string: protein name, gene symbol, or UniProt ID), organism (string, optional, e.g., "Homo sapiens")

Returns: Protein name, function, domains, expression levels, disease associations, subcellular location

Query Types:

  • P12345 — UniProt Accession Number
  • BRCA1_HUMAN — Gene symbol and organism
  • breast cancer type 1 — Protein description
  • TP53 — Gene symbol (organism inferred)

Example Queries

Look up a protein and its domains

"What are the domains and functional regions of the human BRCA1 protein?"

Tool: uniprot_search with query="BRCA1" and organism="Homo sapiens"

Find disease-associated proteins

"Which proteins are associated with cystic fibrosis?"

Tool: uniprot_search with query="cystic fibrosis" and organism="Homo sapiens"

Identify protein-coding variants

"What are the known variants of the TP53 protein in UniProt?"

Tool: uniprot_search with query="TP53" to get variants and sequence positions

Check subcellular location

"Where in the cell is the EGFR protein localized?"

Tool: uniprot_search with query="EGFR" to retrieve location and transmembrane features

Protein Annotation Details

Domains and Motifs

UniProt annotates protein domains using InterPro, Pfam, SMART, and other databases. Each domain is mapped to amino acid positions and functional significance.

Post-Translational Modifications (PTMs)

Key PTM types include:

  • Phosphorylation: Serine, threonine, tyrosine residues
  • Ubiquitination: Lysine residues (protein degradation)
  • Glycosylation: Asparagine (N-linked) or serine/threonine (O-linked)
  • Sumoylation: Lysine residues (regulatory)
  • Acetylation: Lysine residues (gene regulation)

Expression Information

UniProt includes tissue and condition-specific expression data for many proteins, integrated from RNA-seq and microarray studies.

Integration with Datasets MCP

UniProt works seamlessly with other bioinformatics tools:

  • With NCBI Datasets: Get gene info, then fetch protein details from UniProt
  • With Ensembl: Find gene variants, then map them to protein domains using UniProt
  • With PDB: Get protein sequence from UniProt, then find 3D structures
  • With ClinVar: Link clinical variants to protein regions and domains
