Overview

ENCODE (Encyclopedia of DNA Elements) is an international collaborative project that aims to identify and characterize all functional elements in the human genome. It provides comprehensive ChIP-seq datasets for transcription factors and histone modifications, ATAC-seq for chromatin accessibility, RNA-seq for gene expression, and many other assay types across diverse cell types and tissues.

Functional Genomics — ENCODE datasets reveal transcription factor binding sites, chromatin states, gene regulatory elements, and cell-type-specific regulatory landscapes across hundreds of experiments.

Key Features

  • ChIP-seq datasets for transcription factors
  • Histone modification ChIP-seq data
  • ATAC-seq chromatin accessibility data
  • RNA-seq gene expression data
  • DNase-seq open chromatin profiles
  • Peak calls and processed files

Use Cases

  • Find transcription factor binding sites
  • Identify gene regulatory elements
  • Analyze chromatin accessibility
  • Discover cell-type-specific enhancers
  • Map chromatin states and domains
  • Correlate regulatory elements with variants

ENCODE Experiment Retrieval and Analysis

encode_get_experiments

Query ENCODE experiments and retrieve ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq, and RNA-seq datasets.

Parameters: gene (string: gene symbol or genomic region), experiment_type (string, optional: ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq, RNA-seq, DNase-seq)

Returns: Experiment accessions, peak calls, processed files, file URLs, metadata, quality metrics

Experiment Types:

  • ChIP-seq — Transcription factor and histone modification binding
  • ATAC-seq — Chromatin accessibility profiling
  • DNase-seq — Digital DNase hypersensitivity
  • RNA-seq — Gene expression measurements
  • RAMPAGE — Promoter identification
  • Rampage — 5' cap analysis

Example Queries

Find transcription factor binding sites

"Show me ChIP-seq data for TP53 binding sites across cell types."

Tool: encode_get_experiments with gene="TP53", experiment_type="ChIP-seq"

Analyze chromatin accessibility

"Retrieve ATAC-seq data for the BRCA1 region to identify open chromatin."

Tool: encode_get_experiments with gene="BRCA1", experiment_type="ATAC-seq"

Identify regulatory elements

"What enhancers and regulatory elements are associated with the CFTR gene?"

Tool: encode_get_experiments with gene="CFTR"

Examine histone modifications

"Find H3K27ac ChIP-seq data for active enhancers in the MYC locus."

Tool: encode_get_experiments with gene="MYC", experiment_type="ChIP-seq"

Search Strategies

Transcription Factor Mapping

Find all ChIP-seq experiments for a specific transcription factor across cell types and tissues. Identify cell-type-specific and shared binding sites.

Regulatory Element Discovery

  • Use ATAC-seq to identify open chromatin regions
  • Correlate with transcription factor binding
  • Map histone modifications to regulatory states

Cell Type-Specific Analysis

Filter experiments by cell type, tissue, or disease state to understand cell-type-specific gene regulation.

Integration with Variant Data

Map disease-associated variants to regulatory elements identified in ENCODE, linking variants to functional consequences.

Integration with Datasets MCP

ENCODE enriches regulatory genomics research:

  • With Ensembl: Map regulatory elements to gene coordinates
  • With ClinVar: Link disease variants to regulatory elements
  • With GTEx: Correlate expression with regulatory variation
  • With GnomAD: Investigate variant pathogenicity in regulatory regions
