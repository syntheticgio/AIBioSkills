NCBI ClinVar

Clinical Variant Interpretations, Disease Associations, and Pathogenicity Assertions

Home › Databases › NCBI ClinVar

Overview

NCBI ClinVar is a freely accessible, public archive of relationships among variations in human DNA sequences and phenotypes. It aggregates clinical and functional interpretations of genetic variants from the biomedical literature and submitter organizations. ClinVar provides evidence-based classifications for pathogenic, benign, and uncertain variants across diseases.

Curated Clinical Data — ClinVar compiles assertions from clinical genetics labs, research groups, and major databases with evidence supporting interpretations of pathogenic, benign, or uncertain classification.

Key Features

  • Variant pathogenicity classifications
  • Disease associations and phenotypes
  • Assertion evidence levels (gold star, criteria provided)
  • Multiple clinical assertion sources
  • Variant consequences and functional impact
  • Allele frequency data

Use Cases

  • Assess pathogenicity of a clinical variant
  • Find all variants associated with a disease
  • Filter variants by evidence quality (stars)
  • Identify conflicting interpretations
  • Support clinical variant interpretation
  • Analyze disease-gene relationships

Variant Search & Classification

clinvar_search

Search for clinical variants by name, location, or gene.

Parameters: variant (string: rsID, HGVS, or location), gene (string, optional)

Returns: Significance, disease associations, assertion level, evidence quality, submitter info

Significance Values: Pathogenic, Likely Pathogenic, Uncertain Significance, Likely Benign, Benign

Assertion Levels: Gold Star (expert curation), Criteria Provided (explicit ACMG criteria), Conflicting Interpretations

Example Queries

Assess pathogenicity of a known variant

"What is the clinical significance of rs1234567? Is it pathogenic?"

Tool: clinvar_search with variant="rs1234567"

Find variants in a disease gene

"What pathogenic variants have been reported in the BRCA1 gene?"

Tool: clinvar_search with gene="BRCA1"

Search by HGVS notation

"What does ClinVar say about the variant NM_007294.3:c.68_69delAG in BRCA1?"

Tool: clinvar_search with variant="NM_007294.3:c.68_69delAG"

Find high-confidence assertions

"Show me only gold-star variants associated with familial hypercholesterolemia."

Tool: clinvar_search with gene and filter by assertion level

Clinical Interpretation Framework

ClinVar integrates with the American College of Medical Genetics (ACMG) classification system:

  • Pathogenic: Strong evidence of disease causation
  • Likely Pathogenic: Probable disease association
  • Uncertain Significance: Insufficient or conflicting evidence
  • Likely Benign: Probable lack of impact
  • Benign: No evidence of pathogenicity

Evidence Quality (Stars)

  • ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Four stars: Expert curation with detailed review
  • ⭐⭐⭐ Three stars: Criteria provided, multiple submitter agreement
  • ⭐⭐ Two stars: Criteria provided, single submitter
  • ⭐ One star: Submitted without explicit criteria
  • No stars: Conflicting interpretations or insufficient data

Integration with Datasets MCP

ClinVar works seamlessly with other tools in the MCP server:

  • With NCBI Datasets: Get gene info, then query ClinVar for disease variants
  • With Ensembl: Find variants in a region with Ensembl, then assess clinical significance with ClinVar
  • With gnomAD: Cross-reference allele frequencies and clinical assertions
  • With UniProt: Connect protein domains to clinically significant variants
