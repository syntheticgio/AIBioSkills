NCBI Gene Links

Gene Relationships, Networks, and Functional Associations

Home › Databases › NCBI Gene Links

Overview

NCBI Gene Links provides comprehensive information about relationships between genes, including orthologous genes across species, paralogous relationships, gene neighborhoods, and functional associations. The database integrates homology information from multiple sources and computational predictions to reveal gene networks and evolutionary relationships.

Network Analysis — Gene Links enables identification of functionally related genes, exploration of orthologous relationships across organisms, and discovery of gene clusters with coordinated function or regulation.

Key Features

  • Orthologous gene identification across species
  • Paralogous relationships and gene families
  • Gene neighborhood analysis
  • Functional association networks
  • Gene synteny visualization
  • Cross-species gene mapping

Use Cases

  • Find orthologs of a human gene in model organisms
  • Identify paralogous genes with similar functions
  • Analyze gene neighborhoods and chromosomal clusters
  • Discover functionally related genes
  • Explore evolutionary relationships
  • Map genes between species

MCP Tools in Datasets MCP Server

Gene Relationship and Network Analysis

ncbi_gene_links

Query gene relationships, orthologs, and functional associations.

Parameters: gene_id (string: NCBI Gene ID or symbol), organism (string, optional: species name, default Human)

Returns: Related genes, association types, functional categories, species mapping, homology relationships, confidence scores

Association Types:

  • ortholog — Gene in another species derived from common ancestor
  • paralog — Related gene from duplication event
  • neighbor — Adjacent gene on same chromosome
  • functional — Genes in same pathway or GO category
  • interaction — Protein-protein interaction partners
  • synteny — Conserved gene order across species

Example Queries

Find orthologs across species

"What are the orthologs of human BRCA1 in mouse, zebrafish, and fruit fly?"

Tool: ncbi_gene_links with gene_id="BRCA1"

Identify paralogous genes

"Show me all paralogous genes related to CFTR in the human genome."

Tool: ncbi_gene_links with gene_id="CFTR", organism="Homo sapiens"

Analyze gene neighborhoods

"What genes are located near TP53 on chromosome 17?"

Tool: ncbi_gene_links with gene_id="TP53"

Discover functional relationships

"Which genes are functionally related to BRCA2 based on pathways and GO annotations?"

Tool: ncbi_gene_links with gene_id="BRCA2"

Search Strategies

Cross-Species Comparisons

Identify orthologous genes across mammalian species (human, mouse, rat), model organisms (C. elegans, Drosophila, zebrafish), and plants for evolutionary studies and functional characterization.

Gene Family Analysis

  • Retrieve all paralogous genes from gene duplication events
  • Analyze domain structure conservation
  • Identify functionally divergent family members

Neighborhood Genomics

Examine genes in syntenic blocks, identify co-regulated gene clusters, and discover genes with similar evolutionary history.

Functional Clustering

Link genes through shared GO terms, KEGG pathways, Reactome pathways, and computational interaction predictions.

Integration with Datasets MCP

Gene Links enhances comparative genomics and network analysis:

  • With Ensembl: Map orthologs and explore syntenic regions
  • With KEGG/Reactome: Analyze genes in conserved pathways
  • With STRING: Investigate protein-protein interaction networks
  • With NCBI Datasets: Retrieve genomic coordinates for related genes
