PharmGKB

Pharmacogenomics Knowledge Base with Drug-Gene-Phenotype Relationships

Home › Databases › PharmGKB

Overview

PharmGKB (Pharmacogenomics Knowledge Base) is a comprehensive pharmacogenomics resource that catalogs relationships between drugs, genes, and phenotypes. It provides evidence-based knowledge about how genetic variation affects drug metabolism, efficacy, and adverse effects, supporting clinical decision-making and precision medicine initiatives.

Precision Pharmacotherapy — PharmGKB integrates drug-gene interactions with clinical guidelines, enabling pharmacogenomic assessment to personalize drug selection and dosing.

Key Features

  • Drug-gene interactions
  • Pharmacogenomic pathways
  • Clinical practice guidelines
  • Dosing recommendations
  • Adverse reaction data
  • Functional variant info

Pharmacogenomic Search and Analysis

pharmgkb_search

Search drug-gene interactions and pharmacogenomic relationships.

Parameters: drug (string: drug name or identifier), gene (string, optional: gene symbol or Ensembl ID)

Returns: Drug-gene interactions, phenotypes, clinical significance, dosing recommendations, variant effects, guidelines

Major Drug-Metabolizing Gene Families:

  • Cytochrome P450 (CYP): CYP2D6, CYP2C19, CYP2C9, CYP3A4/5
  • Phase II Enzymes: NAT2, TPMT, COMT, UGT1A1
  • Transporters: MDR1/ABCB1, OATP1B1/1B3
  • Drug Targets: VKORC1, F5, F2, ITPA

Example Queries

Find drug response variants

"What genetic variants in CYP2D6 affect codeine metabolism and response?"

Tool: pharmgkb_search with drug="codeine" and gene="CYP2D6"

Look up clinical guidelines

"What are the PharmGKB clinical guidelines for warfarin dosing based on genotype?"

Tool: pharmgkb_search with drug="warfarin"

Assess drug response

"What is the relationship between TPMT variants and azathioprine response?"

Tool: pharmgkb_search with drug="azathioprine" and gene="TPMT"

Find adverse reaction risks

"Which genetic variants increase the risk of adverse reactions to NSAIDs?"

Tool: pharmgkb_search with drug="NSAID" or specific NSAID names

Pharmacogenomic Pathways

Key Drug Metabolism Pathways

PharmGKB organizes information around major pharmacogenomic pathways relevant to clinical practice:

  • Warfarin Metabolism: VKORC1 and CYP2C9 variants affecting anticoagulation
  • Clopidogrel Activation: CYP2C19 variants affecting platelet inhibition
  • Thiopurine Metabolism: TPMT variants affecting azathioprine and 6-MP toxicity
  • HLA-B*5701: Abacavir hypersensitivity in HIV-positive individuals
  • DPYD Deficiency: 5-Fluorouracil toxicity risk assessment
  • Codeine Response: CYP2D6 variants affecting pain relief

Evidence Levels

PharmGKB rates evidence supporting drug-gene relationships:

  • 1A: Clinical practice guidelines and FDA labeling
  • 1B: Important relationship supported by strong evidence
  • 2A: Moderate evidence, some clinical validation
  • 2B: Emerging evidence, research phase

Clinical Implementation

Dosing Recommendations

PharmGKB provides evidence-based dosing adjustments based on individual pharmacogenotypes, helping clinicians optimize therapy and minimize adverse effects. Recommendations are regularly updated as new evidence emerges.

FDA Pharmacogenomic Labeling

PharmGKB tracks FDA drug labels containing pharmacogenomic information, indicating FDA-recognized relationships between genetic variation and drug response. This supports regulatory compliance and informed prescribing decisions.

Integration with Datasets MCP

PharmGKB data enhances clinical and research applications:

  • With Ensembl: Map genetic variants to drug-metabolizing genes
  • With ClinVar: Assess clinical significance of variants in PharmGKB pathways
  • With UniProt: Understand protein functional effects of variants
  • With PubMed: Find supporting literature for pharmacogenomic associations
