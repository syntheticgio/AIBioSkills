GTEx

Tissue-Specific Gene Expression and eQTL Data from 53 Human Tissues

Home › Databases › GTEx

Overview

The Genotype-Tissue Expression (GTEx) project provides a comprehensive resource of tissue-specific gene expression and expression quantitative trait loci (eQTLs) across 53 human tissues. Based on RNA-seq data from nearly 1,000 individuals, GTEx enables understanding of how genetic variants affect gene expression in different tissues and tissues-specific regulatory mechanisms.

Multi-tissue RNA-seq — GTEx generates transcriptomic data from diverse human tissues, revealing tissue-specific gene regulation and the effects of genetic variation on expression.

Key Features

  • 53 tissue types surveyed
  • TPM expression values
  • eQTL associations
  • 1000+ individual donors
  • Tissue-specific regulation
  • Allele-specific expression

Use Cases

  • Find tissue-specific expression
  • Study eQTL associations
  • Understand variant effects
  • Identify disease-relevant tissues
  • Validate expression predictions
  • Prioritize disease genes

Gene Expression & eQTL Queries

gtex_get_expression

Retrieve tissue-specific expression and eQTL associations for genes.

Parameters: gene (string: gene name or Ensembl ID), tissue (string, optional: specific tissue or "all")

Returns: TPM expression values by tissue, tissue-specific eQTLs, effect sizes, allele frequencies, significance scores

Tissue Categories:

  • Brain: Frontal cortex, anterior cingulate cortex, hippocampus, etc.
  • Heart: Left ventricle, atrial appendage
  • GI Tract: Esophagus, stomach, small intestine, colon
  • Immune: Blood, thymus, spleen
  • Endocrine: Pancreas, thyroid, adrenal gland, pituitary
  • Other: Liver, kidney, lung, muscle, skin, adipose

Example Queries

Get tissue-specific expression

"What is the expression level of BRCA1 in brain versus breast tissue according to GTEx?"

Understanding GTEx Data

Gene Expression Units (TPM)

GTEx reports expression in Transcripts Per Million (TPM), a normalized measure enabling comparison across genes and samples. TPM values are normalized by gene length and total sequencing depth in each sample.

Expression Quantitative Trait Loci (eQTLs)

eQTLs are genetic variants that significantly affect gene expression. GTEx provides:

  • Cis-eQTLs: Variants near the gene (< 1 Mb) affecting local expression
  • Trans-eQTLs: Distant variants affecting expression (detected in GTEx)
  • Effect Sizes: Slope and confidence intervals for allele effects
  • Tissue-specificity: Which tissues show eQTL associations

Tissue Specificity

GTEx identifies tissue-specific expression patterns, revealing which tissues have high or low expression for each gene. This helps prioritize tissues for disease studies and drug development.

Integration with Datasets MCP

GTEx data enhances functional analysis across the datasets ecosystem:

  • With Ensembl: Link genetic variants to tissue-specific expression changes
  • With ClinVar: Assess how disease variants affect tissue expression
  • With GWAS: Identify disease variants through eQTL associations
  • With UniProt: Correlate protein expression with tissue-specific transcription
