Overview

PubMed Central (PMC) is the free full-text archive of biomedical and life sciences journals at the U.S. National Institutes of Health's National Library of Medicine. It provides open access to full-text articles from participating journals, author manuscripts, and free PMC articles, enabling comprehensive text-mining and detailed research discovery beyond abstract-level searching available in PubMed.

Open Science — With over 10 million full-text articles freely available, PMC enables in-depth literature analysis, figure extraction, supplementary data access, and comprehensive text-mining for research synthesis.

Key Features

  • Full-text article access and search
  • Structured XML/HTML formats
  • Supplementary materials and data
  • Figure and table extraction
  • Author manuscripts availability
  • Text-mining and analytics

Use Cases

  • Extract gene/protein mentions from full text
  • Access supplementary data and methods
  • Analyze figures and tables in publications
  • Mine methods sections for reproducibility
  • Retrieve complete research datasets
  • Conduct comprehensive literature reviews

Search Strategies

Full-Text Mining

Search full-text articles instead of just abstracts to discover mentions of genes, proteins, and diseases throughout the paper, increasing discovery sensitivity.

Supplementary Data Access

  • Access raw data files and datasets
  • Retrieve extended methods and protocols
  • Get additional figures and analysis results

Structured Content Extraction

Use XML-structured formats to extract specific sections (methods, results, discussion) programmatically for comprehensive literature synthesis.

Figure and Caption Analysis

Extract figures and captions for visual data analysis, chart digitization, and image-based discovery.

Integration with Datasets MCP

PubMed Central enriches research workflows with full-text resources:

  • With PubMed: Discover papers in abstract search, then retrieve full text from PMC
  • With NCBI Datasets: Find gene papers, access full-text validation studies
  • With ClinVar: Retrieve supporting literature for variant interpretations
  • With UniProt: Access protein studies with complete methodological details
