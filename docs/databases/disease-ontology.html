Disease Ontology

Standardized Disease Terminology, Hierarchies, and Disease-Gene Associations

Home › Databases › Disease Ontology

Overview

The Disease Ontology (DO) is a standardized, hierarchically organized vocabulary of human diseases. It provides disease definitions, disease-disease relationships, and cross-links to disease databases including OMIM, MeSH, ICD-9, and ICD-10. The DO integrates disease nomenclature with genetic information to support computational disease analysis, machine learning applications, and clinical research.

Standardized Terminology — Disease Ontology provides a unified classification system for over 12,000 human diseases, enabling consistent disease annotation, computational analysis, and integration with genetic and phenotypic data.

Key Features

  • Comprehensive disease vocabulary
  • Hierarchical disease classification
  • Disease-disease relationships
  • Cross-references to standard databases
  • Disease-gene associations
  • Standardized disease identifiers

Use Cases

Disease Terminology and Classification

disease_ontology_search

Search Disease Ontology for standardized disease terms and disease-gene associations.

Parameters: disease_term (string: disease name or synonym), gene (string, optional: associated gene symbol)

Returns: Disease ID, definition, parent/child terms, synonyms, cross-references, associated genes, confidence scores

Search Examples:

  • disease_term="breast cancer" — Find standard breast cancer term
  • disease_term="cystic fibrosis", gene="CFTR" — Disease-gene association
  • disease_term="neurodegenerative disease" — Find parent category
  • gene="TP53" — Find all diseases associated with TP53

Disease-Gene Network Analysis

Integrate disease ontology terms with gene annotations to build disease-specific gene networks, enabling systems biology and computational drug discovery.

Cross-Database Integration

Link Disease Ontology IDs to OMIM, MeSH, SNOMED CT, and ICD codes for integration with clinical, research, and administrative systems.
