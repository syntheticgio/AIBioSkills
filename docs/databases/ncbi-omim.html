Overview

OMIM (Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man) is a comprehensive database of human genes and genetic disorders. It catalogs known Mendelian diseases and phenotypes, providing detailed gene-disease associations, inheritance patterns, clinical features, and molecular mechanisms for over 25,000 entries covering both genes and hereditary phenotypes.

Clinical Relevance — OMIM is essential for understanding single-gene disorders, establishing genotype-phenotype correlations, and identifying disease-causing variants in the context of known genetic diseases.

Key Features

  • Gene-disease association curation
  • Phenotype and inheritance pattern documentation
  • Clinical features and diagnostic criteria
  • Molecular basis of genetic diseases
  • References to primary literature
  • Cross-links to other genetic databases

Use Cases

  • Identify diseases associated with a gene
  • Understand phenotype-genotype relationships
  • Determine inheritance patterns
  • Find clinical diagnostic criteria
  • Discover molecular mechanisms of disease
  • Retrieve curated disease information

Gene-Disease and Phenotype Search

omim_search

Search OMIM for gene-disease associations and phenotype information.

Parameters: gene (string: gene symbol or name), disease (string, optional: disease name or OMIM ID)

Returns: Gene ID, associated diseases/phenotypes, inheritance patterns, clinical features, OMIM MIM numbers, references

Search Examples:

  • gene="BRCA1" — Find all BRCA1-associated diseases
  • gene="TP53", disease="Li-Fraumeni" — Gene-specific disease search
  • disease="cystic fibrosis" — Find genes associated with a disease
  • gene="CFTR", disease="pancreatic disease" — Detailed phenotype association

Example Queries

Find diseases associated with a gene

"What genetic diseases are associated with the BRCA1 gene?"

Tool: omim_search with gene="BRCA1"

Identify inheritance patterns

"What is the inheritance pattern for cystic fibrosis mutations in the CFTR gene?"

Tool: omim_search with gene="CFTR", disease="cystic fibrosis"

Understand phenotype-genotype relationships

"Show me the clinical features of Marfan syndrome associated with FBN1 mutations."

Tool: omim_search with gene="FBN1", disease="Marfan syndrome"

Retrieve genes for a disease

"Which genes are responsible for familial hypercholesterolemia?"

Tool: omim_search with disease="familial hypercholesterolemia"

Search Strategies

By Gene Symbol

Search by standard gene symbols (e.g., "BRCA1", "TP53", "CFTR") to retrieve all associated genetic disorders. OMIM integrates with NCBI Gene for standardized nomenclature.

By Disease Name

  • Use exact disease names or OMIM MIM numbers
  • Search for phenotype descriptions
  • Retrieve inheritance pattern information

By Inheritance Pattern

Filter results by inheritance mode: autosomal dominant (AD), autosomal recessive (AR), X-linked (XL), or mitochondrial (MT).

Cross-Database Links

OMIM entries link to ClinVar, UniProt, Ensembl, and other databases for comprehensive disease variant analysis.

Integration with Datasets MCP

OMIM data enriches genetic analysis workflows:

  • With ClinVar: Annotate variants with disease associations from OMIM
  • With Ensembl: Identify disease-causing variants in known genes
  • With UniProt: Correlate protein function with genetic disease mechanisms
  • With HPO: Map phenotypes to genetic disorders
