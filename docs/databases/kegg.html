Overview

KEGG (Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes) is an integrated database resource for systems biology and functional genomics. It provides manually curated pathway maps for metabolic processes, signaling cascades, disease mechanisms, and drug targets across organisms. KEGG integrates genomic, chemical, and systems biology data.

Curated Pathway Data — KEGG maintains expert-curated pathway maps showing molecular interactions and reactions with links to genes, proteins, compounds, and diseases.

Key Features

  • Metabolic pathway maps
  • Signaling cascade diagrams
  • Gene to pathway mapping
  • Disease-pathway associations
  • Drug-target information
  • Organism-specific pathways

Use Cases

  • Find pathways involving a gene
  • Explore metabolic routes
  • Identify disease mechanisms
  • Discover drug targets
  • Analyze pathway perturbations
  • Study multi-gene interactions

Pathway Retrieval

kegg_get_pathway

Retrieve pathway information, genes, and metabolic details.

Parameters: pathway_id (string: KEGG pathway ID, e.g., hsa00010)

Returns: Pathway name, description, genes, enzymes, compounds, reactions, disease associations

Pathway ID Format: Three-letter organism code + 5-digit pathway number

  • hsa00010 — Glycolysis/Gluconeogenesis (Homo sapiens)
  • hsa05200 — Pathways in cancer (disease)
  • hsa01100 — Metabolic pathways (overview)
  • mmu00010 — Glycolysis (Mus musculus)

Example Queries

Explore a metabolic pathway

"Show me the glycolysis pathway in humans. What genes and enzymes are involved?"

Pathway Categories

Metabolism (00000-01999)

  • Carbohydrate, lipid, amino acid, and nucleotide metabolism
  • Energy metabolism (glycolysis, TCA cycle, oxidative phosphorylation)
  • Secondary metabolism and cofactor biosynthesis

Genetic Information Processing (02000-02299)

  • Transcription, translation, replication
  • RNA processing and protein folding

Signaling & Cell Communication (04000-04999)

  • MAPK, Wnt, Notch, TGF-beta signaling
  • Cell cycle, apoptosis, immune response
  • Growth factor and hormone signaling

Human Diseases (05000-05999)

  • Cancer pathways and mechanisms
  • Infectious disease pathways
  • Immune and metabolic disease pathways

Enzyme & Reaction Information

EC Numbers

KEGG uses Enzyme Commission (EC) numbering to classify enzymatic reactions:

  • EC 1: Oxidoreductases (oxidation-reduction reactions)
  • EC 2: Transferases (group transfer reactions)
  • EC 3: Hydrolases (hydrolysis reactions)
  • EC 4: Lyases (non-hydrolytic bond breakage)
  • EC 5: Isomerases (rearrangement reactions)
  • EC 6: Ligases (bond formation)

Reaction Details

Each pathway reaction includes substrates, products, enzymes, and cofactors, allowing detailed analysis of metabolic transformations.

Integration with Datasets MCP

KEGG pathways integrate with systems biology and functional genomics analysis:

  • With NCBI Datasets: Get gene info, then explore KEGG pathways
  • With STRING: Combine protein interaction networks with pathway context
  • With UniProt: Link protein functions to metabolic pathways
  • With ClinVar: Map disease variants to disrupted pathways
