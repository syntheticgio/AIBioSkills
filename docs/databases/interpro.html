InterPro

Protein Domains, Families, and Functional Sites Database

Home › Databases › InterPro

Overview

InterPro is the integrated protein signature database that provides comprehensive annotations for protein domains, families, and functional sites. It integrates curated signatures from multiple databases including Pfam, SMART, ProDom, PRINTS, ProSite, TIGRFAMs, and SUPERFAMILY, providing a unified platform for protein classification and functional annotation.

Unified Classification — InterPro combines signature methods from multiple sources, allowing comprehensive protein domain detection and family classification across all organisms.

Key Features

  • Integrated protein signatures
  • Domain and family classification
  • Functional site prediction
  • Multi-database integration
  • GO term annotations
  • Pathway associations

Use Cases

  • Identify protein domains and motifs
  • Classify uncharacterized proteins
  • Find functional sites and binding regions
  • Study protein evolution
  • Predict protein function from sequence
  • Map disease variants to domains

Integrated Signature Databases

Database Overview

InterPro integrates signatures from seven major databases:

  • Pfam: Protein families based on hidden Markov models (HMMs)
  • SMART: Domain sequence alignments and HMMS for modular proteins
  • ProDom: Protein domain families derived from clustering
  • PRINTS: Fingerprints—sets of conserved motifs
  • ProSite: Patterns and profiles for functional sites
  • TIGRFAMs: Hidden Markov models for protein families and domains
  • SUPERFAMILY: Structural domain classification based on SCOP

Domain and Family Search

interpro_get_domains

Identify protein domains, families, and functional sites in a protein sequence.

Parameters: protein (string: protein sequence or identifier), uniprot_id (string, optional)

Returns: Domain name, identifier, position range, E-value, database source, GO annotations, confidence score

Input Options:

  • Raw amino acid sequence (FASTA format)
  • UniProt accession number (e.g., P12345)
  • UniProt entry name (e.g., TP53_HUMAN)
  • Gene symbol with organism

Domain Architecture & Classification

Understanding Domain Hits

Each domain hit includes position information (start and end amino acids), allowing you to map domain structure along the protein sequence and identify multi-domain architectures common in modular proteins.

E-value Interpretation

  • < 1e-10: Highly significant hits, reliable domain prediction
  • 1e-10 to 1e-5: Significant hits, good confidence
  • 1e-5 to 0.01: Moderate confidence, consider context
  • > 0.01: Weak hits, likely false positives

GO Term Annotations

InterPro-identified domains are linked to Gene Ontology terms, providing functional predictions at three levels: biological process, molecular function, and cellular component.

Integration with Datasets MCP

InterPro domains enhance analysis across the datasets ecosystem:

  • With UniProt: Correlate curated domain annotations with InterPro predictions
  • With PDB: Map InterPro domains to known protein structures
  • With Ensembl: Link genomic variants to predicted functional domains
  • With ClinVar: Assess impact of variants on domain integrity
