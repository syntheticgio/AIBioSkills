Overview

COSMIC (Catalogue of Somatic Mutations in Cancer) is the world's largest and most comprehensive resource of somatic mutations in human cancer. It provides detailed information about mutations found in cancer tissues, including frequency, cancer types affected, and functional consequences. COSMIC enables researchers to understand cancer-specific mutation patterns and identify oncogenes and tumor suppressors.

Cancer-Centric Mutation Database — COSMIC specializes in somatic mutations found in cancers, capturing disease-specific mutation frequencies and cancer-associated mutation hotspots.

Key Features

  • Somatic cancer mutations
  • Cancer type associations
  • Mutation frequencies
  • Functional consequences
  • Tissue origin mapping
  • Sample metadata

Use Cases

Cancer Mutation Search and Analysis

cosmic_search

Search for somatic mutations in cancer samples and retrieve cancer-specific data.

Parameters: gene (string: gene symbol or Ensembl ID), cancer_type (string, optional: specific cancer type or "all")

Returns: Mutation details (position, nucleotide change, protein change), cancer type frequencies, sample count, tissue origin, functional impact, driver status

Major Cancer Types:

  • Lung cancer (small cell, non-small cell)
  • Breast cancer (ductal, lobular)
  • Colorectal cancer
  • Melanoma
  • Leukemia (AML, ALL, CML)
  • Lymphoma (Hodgkin, non-Hodgkin)
  • Ovarian cancer
  • Liver cancer
  • Pancreatic cancer
  • And many others...

Somatic Mutation Analysis

Mutation Frequency & Recurrence

COSMIC tracks how frequently specific mutations occur across cancer patients, helping identify driver mutations that confer selective advantage in cancer development. Recurrent mutations at the same position across multiple patients indicate strong cancer association.

Mutation Consequence Types

  • Missense: Amino acid substitution, often affecting protein function
  • Nonsense: Stop codon creation, premature termination
  • Frameshift: Insertions/deletions causing reading frame shift
  • Splice Site: Affects mRNA splicing, may alter protein
  • Silent: Synonymous change, typically no functional effect
  • Inframe Indel: Insertion/deletion maintaining reading frame

Cancer-Specific Patterns

Different cancer types show characteristic mutation signatures. For example:

  • Lung Cancer: High frequency of KRAS and TP53 mutations; smoking-associated patterns
  • Breast Cancer: TP53, GATA3, and PIK3CA mutations common
  • Melanoma: BRAF V600E mutation extremely frequent; UV-induced signatures
  • Leukemia: Gene fusions and chromosomal translocations characteristic

Driver vs. Passenger Mutations

Classification

COSMIC distinguishes between:

  • Driver Mutations: Confer selective advantage in cancer development, often found in oncogenes or tumor suppressors
  • Passenger Mutations: Occur in cancer but don't directly contribute to transformation
  • CGC Annotation: Cross-reference with Cancer Gene Census for known cancer-associated genes

Functional Impact Assessment

COSMIC provides information about functional consequences of mutations, helping prioritize mutations for experimental and clinical investigation.

Integration with Datasets MCP

COSMIC data enhances cancer genomics and clinical research:

  • With Ensembl: Map COSMIC mutations to genomic coordinates and genes
  • With ClinVar: Compare somatic and germline mutation annotations
  • With UniProt: Understand functional consequences at protein level
  • With GTEx: Correlate cancer mutations with tissue-specific expression
